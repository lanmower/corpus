## Lecture Notes

### Course Overview

The CMED5CH module is an expansion of knowledge and skills in child health, instructed by Dr F Naby. The course is divided into two main components: neonatal care and general paediatrics, each with distinct objectives and clinical placements.

### Neonatal Course Objectives

Students must be competent in taking an appropriate history for the neonate and performing a thorough examination of the newborn with specific focus on gestational age assessment using the Ballard score. The course emphasizes two key principles of neonatal resuscitation: KINC (a specific resuscitation protocol) and Kangaroo Mother Care. Students must recognize the three classifications of low birth weight babies: LBW (Low Birth Weight), VLBW (Very Low Birth Weight), and ELBW (Extremely Low Birth Weight). Understanding feeding methods and volumes used in neonatal care is essential, as is familiarity with common infections and conditions occurring in the neonate.

### Paediatrics Course Objectives

Students must be able to take an appropriate history for the clinical problem and undertake an examination of the relevant system involved. A critical objective is to recognize the features and understand the principles of management of common disorders. Students must understand triage and resuscitation of sick children, and develop communication skills with caregivers including counseling abilities. Finally, students must observe and/or perform the designated procedures as required.

### Course Structure

The course is structured as essentially lecture week conducted online through a hybrid portal with videos uploaded for asynchronous access. Clinical exposure is divided into 2 weeks of neonatal exposure and 2 weeks of paediatrics (4 weeks total). Clinical placement occurs at five hospitals in PMB: Edendale hospital, Greys, Northdale (Paeds), Queen Nandhi, and Newcastle complex. Students will be divided equally between Greys/NDH and Edendale Hospital only in PMB.

### Clinical Placement Requirements and Safety

During clinical placement, students must wear masks, abide to social distancing, and practice hand hygiene. A critical policy states: DO NOT PRESENT TO THE CLINICAL SETTING IF YOU ARE NOT WELL; daily symptom tool monitoring is required. Students must remind the registrars and medical officers on the date and time of tutorial at the beginning of the week to ensure tutorials are scheduled appropriately.

### Wardwork and Tutorials

Students should not cohort and group clerk. Students are allowed to clerk patients and participate in tutorials but must adhere to designated areas. The consultant in charge assists to oversee this process. All tutorials will be conducted at bedside. Additionally, students will now participate in intakes.

### Assessments and Grading

Assessment in this module comprises formative and summative components with a clear breakdown. Formative assessments account for 20% of the final mark and include in-block assessment of a learning portfolio of clinical cases, in-block assessment of clinical case (conducted in-person), and professionalism score. Students must write 2 learning portfolios of clinical cases: 1 paediatric and 1 neonatal. The first portfolio must be submitted on Thursday of week 2 (marked for in-block assessment). The second learning portfolio (1 neonatal or 1 paediatric case) is submitted for final assessment and the assessment will be virtual.

Summative assessments consist of two components: Clinical examination accounts for 40% of the total mark and consists of 2 clinical cases and 1 portfolio case. Theory examination accounts for 40% of the total mark and is a paper-based structured clinical examination (MCQ single best answer).

The overall assessment breakdown by percentage is as follows: Formative 20% (portfolio, clinical case, professionalism), Clinical examination 40%, and Theory 40%.

### Pass Marks and Supplementary Examination

The overall pass mark is 50% with a subminimum of 50% separately in the clinical and theory examinations, and a pass in at least 2/3 clinical components. If a student scores 40-50%, this results in a fail, but a supplementary examination will be granted. If a student scores less than 40%, this results in a fail with NO supplementary examination granted. Students eligible for supplementary examination may sit it early next year.
