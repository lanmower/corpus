Hypertension Treatment Strategy
Stepwise
1. Thiazide diuretic Eg hydrochlorothiazide
2. ACE inhibitor or ARB blocker
Enalapril or Lorsartan
3. Calcium channel blocker
Amlodipine
4. Beta blocker
Ejection Fraction > 40% - atenolol
Ejection Fraction < 40% - carvedilol
5. Alpha blocker
Doxazosin or prazocin
6. Direct vasodilator
Hydrallazine
Minoxidil

--- page 17 ---
Special Therapeutic Considerations
Resistant hypertension
Failure to control diastolic < 90mmHg, despite the use of 3 agents including a diuretic
Hypertension in pregnancy
Methyldopa, nifedipine, labetalol
Diabetes Mellitus
Benefit from ACE inhibitors

Hypertensive Crisis
When diastolic BP > 140mmHg,  surge in cerebral blood flow  encephalopathy
Nitroprusside / Nitroglycerin
Acute Left Ventricular failure
Due to high blood pressure
Results in pulmonary oedema and acute respiratory distress
Treatment – diuresis, IV BP lowering agents, low dose iv morphine, oxygen and ventilation if necessary
