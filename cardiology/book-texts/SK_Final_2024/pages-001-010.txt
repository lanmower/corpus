Introduction
Coronary Artery Disease (Ischaemic Heart Disease) is the number 1 cause of death globally, responsible for 16% of the world’s total deaths
CAD is a dynamic process that can be modified by lifestyle, pharmacological therapies and revascularization which can result in disease stabilization or regression

RISK RISK FACTORS: Non-Modifiable
Age:
CAD prevalence increases after 35 years
The lifetime risk of developing CAD in men and women after 40 years of age is 49% and 32%, respectively
Gender: Men are at increased risk compared to women
Ethnicity: Southeast Asians are at an increased risk
Family history:
A family history of CAD < 50 years of age has an increased CAD mortality risk
a father or brother diagnosed with CAD before 55 years of age and a mother or sister diagnosed before 65 years of age are considered risk factors

--- page 7 ---
RISK FACTORS: Non-Modifiable
Hypertension: About 1 out of every three patients have hypertension
Hyperlipidaemia:  the second most common risk factor
Diabetes Mellitus: The heart disease rate is 2.5 times higher in patients with diabetes compared to those without diabetes
Obesity: is an independent risk factor for CAD and also increases the risk of developing other risk factors including hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and diabetes mellitus
Smoking: Non-smokers regularly exposed to second-hand smoke have a 25% to 30% increased risk of CAD compared to those not exposed
Poor diet:
Added Sugar - soft drinks and sweetened beverages are associated with a 22% higher risk of MI
Saturated & trans fats
Red meat or processed meat
Sedentary Lifestyle
