Definition of Hypertension
Usual BP > 140/90
Treatment initiated if BP > 130/80 in high risk patients esp. With coronary artery disease

--- page 3 ---
Essential Hypertension (90%)
Hemodynamic subtypes
Systolic Hypertension in Young Adults
Diastolic hypertension in middle age
Isolated systolic hypertension in older adults
Mechanisms
Neural – sympathetic overactivity
Baroreceptor dysfunction
Obesity related
Obstructive sleep apnoea – increased catecholamines
Renal mechanisms – resetting pressure natriuresis /low birth weight
Endothelial cell dysfunction

--- page 4 ---
Secondary Hypertension (10%)
Renal parenchymal disease
GFR <60ml/min
Acute Kidney diseases – any acute insult
Chronic Kidney disease
Various causes of CKD
Hemodialysis patients
Renal transplant recipients
Renovascular hypertension

Consequences of hypertension
Hypertensive heart disease
Pressure overload hypertrophy
Heart failure, ventricular tachyarrythmias, atrial fibrillation
Myocyte hypertrophy, collagen desposition,  myocardial fibrosis
CNS
ischeamic stroke, embolic stroke
Renal
Vascular system
Retinopathy

--- page 7 ---
Initial Evaluation of patient
Measurement of Blood Pressure
After 5 minutes of rest, back supported in a chair,
 Relaxed state
Measure at least twice
Appropriately sized cuff
Tobacco and cafeine to avoided for 30mins prior
Should measure in both arms, and then after 5mins of standing  exclude postural hypotension
(diabetes/parkinsons/autonomic neuropathy)

Prehypertension	120-139		80-89
Stage 1		140-159		90-99
Stage 2		160-179		100-109
Stage 3		>180			>110
