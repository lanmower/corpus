## Study Strategy for Nephrology Mastery

### Foundation Phase
1. **Nephrology Introduction** - Start here to understand kidney anatomy, physiology, and the clinical syndromes you'll study
2. **Acute Kidney Disease** - Learn the acute presentations, classification (KDIGO), and immediate management
3. **Approach To Chronic Kidney Disease** - Understand CKD staging, pathogenesis, and long-term management

### Clinical Complications Phase
4. **Diabetic Kidney Disease** - The most common cause of CKD globally; learn pathophysiology and management
5. **Glomerular Disease** - Primary and secondary glomerulonephritis; nephrotic vs nephritic syndromes
6. **Hypertension & The Kidney** - Bidirectional relationship: HTN causes kidney disease AND kidney disease causes HTN
7. **HIV & The Kidney** - HIV-associated nephropathy, immune complex disease, drug nephrotoxicity

### Specialized Topics Phase
8. **Urinary Tract Infections (UTI)** - Distinguish upper vs lower UTI; management by infection type
9. **Drugs & The Kidney** - Critical safety: nephrotoxic agents, dosing adjustments, contrast nephropathy
10. **Kidney Replacement Therapy** - Dialysis modalities, transplantation, and end-stage renal disease management

### Active Learning Methods
- **Read actively**: Pause after each section section and summarize key points
- **Cross-reference**: Link topics (e.g., diabetic kidney disease involves hypertension, proteinuria, glomerular damage)
- **Use SRS cards**: Repetition strength system cards are available in `nephrology/srs-cards/` - review daily
- **Apply to cases**: Use the clinical scenarios presented in each section to test understanding
- **Connect pathophysiology**: Every finding has an underlying mechanism - understand the "why"

### Mastery Checklist
- [ ] **Anatomy & Physiology**: Can label kidney structures and explain glomerular filtration
- [ ] **AKI vs CKD**: Understand the distinction, KDIGO classification, and investigation approach
- [ ] **Proteinuria**: Know significance of different levels and what they indicate (UACR, nephrotic range)
- [ ] **Electrolyte Disorders**: Can diagnose and manage hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, hypocalcemia
- [ ] **Glomerulonephritis**: Differentiate between types on clinical presentation, serology, and biopsy
- [ ] **Diabetic Kidney Disease**: Stages of DN, cardiovascular implications, SGLT2i and GLP1-RA benefits
- [ ] **CKD Progression**: Know the modifiable risk factors (BP, proteinuria, glucose) and how to slow progression
- [ ] **Drug Safety**: Recognize nephrotoxic drugs and required dose adjustments at different GFR levels
- [ ] **Dialysis**: Understand hemodialysis vs peritoneal dialysis, access types, and adequacy measures
- [ ] **Clinical Integration**: Can assess a patient with kidney disease systematically (hx, exam, labs, imaging)

### Passing Standard
- SRS card review: >90% passing rate on all flashcard decks
- Practice cases: Apply knowledge to 3+ real patient scenarios per topic
- Integrated knowledge: Can explain relationships between different nephrology topics
- Ready for exams: Able to recognize clinical patterns and manage appropriately
